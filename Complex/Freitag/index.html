Teo I.1.1 (Teorema de existencia de complejos)

There exists a field \(\mathbb{C}\) with the following properties:

  1. The field \(\mathbb{R}\) of real numbers is a subfield of \(\mathbb{C}\), i.e. \(\mathbb{R}\) is a subset of \(\mathbb{C}\), and addition and multiplication in \(\mathbb{R}\) are the restrictions to \(\mathbb{R}\) of the addition and multiplication in \(\mathbb{C}\).

  2. The equation $$X^2 + 1 = 0$$ has exactly two solutions in \(\mathbb{C}\).

  3. Let \(i\) be one of the two solutions; then \(-i\) is the other. The map \begin{equation*} \begin{split} \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} &\to \mathbb{C},\\ (x,y) &\to x+iy, \end{split} \end{equation*} is a bijection.

Def I.1.3 (Definición de módulo de un complejo)

The absolute value or modulus of a complex number \(z\) is defined by $$|z|:= \sqrt{z \bar{z}} = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$$

Teo I.1.5 (Teorema del mapeo polar)

Let \(z \in \mathbb{C}\). Then there exists a complex number \(w \in \mathbb{C}\) such that \(w^2 = z\). (Notice that \(-w\) also satisfies this equation.)
