Def 1.1.1 (Definición complejos tuplas)

The system of complex numbers, denoted \(\mathbb{C}\) is the set \(\mathbb{R}^2\) together with the usual rules of vector addition and scalar multiplication by a real number \(a\), namely, \begin{equation*} \begin{split} (x_1,y_1 ) + (x_2,y_2 ) &= (x_1 + x_2,y_1 + y_2 )\\ a(x,y) &= (ax, ay) \end{split} \end{equation*} and with the operation of complex multiplication, defined by \[(x_1,y_1)(x_2,y_2) = (x_1 x_2 - y_1 y_2,x_1 y_2 + x_2 y_1).\]

Teo 1.1.2

The complex numbers \(\mathbb{C}\) form a field.

Prop 1.1.3 (Proposición raíz de complejos)

Let \(z \in \mathbb{C}\). Then there exists a complex number \(w \in \mathbb{C}\) such that \(w^2 = z\). (Notice that \(-w\) also satisfies this equation.)
